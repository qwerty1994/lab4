Pushkin Savelich naively convinced that the serfs only exist for a lifetime to work for their masters. But his devotion to the Lord is far from slavish humiliation. In response to the harsh, unjust accusations Savelich gentleman wrote to him: "... I am not an old dog, but your faithful servant, obey the master's orders, and you always diligently served and lived to gray hair." Large inner nobility, spiritual richness of nature are fully disclosed in a totally selfless and deeply humane attachment poor, Odie nokogo old man to his pet. "Savelich - a miracle! This person is most tragic, that is a pity that most in the story" - wrote Pushkin V. Odoevsky.
